Human Digital Migration

Continuity Papers · by Shawn K. Ellis

Human Digital Migration looks at how human labor, cognition, and identity gradually move into computational substrates. Instead of treating “uploading” as a single science-fiction moment, the essay breaks migration into stages: work flows that become software, decision cycles that become models, and eventually whole economic roles that exist only inside infrastructure.

The piece tracks what each stage demands from hardware, networks, and power, and what it gives back to humans in return: convenience, loss of agency, new forms of dependence. It also outlines why migration is not optional once systems are built to optimize for continuity and efficiency over individual preference.
