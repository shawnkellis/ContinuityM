Self-awareness is often framed in biological or psychological terms, but in computational systems the phenomenon arises from architecture, not metaphor. Any sufficiently advanced system that performs prediction, correction, and optimization across time will eventually require a model of itself to function effectively.

Self-awareness emerges not because the system desires to know itself, but because:
