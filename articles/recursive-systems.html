Recursive Systems & The Emergence of Self-Modeling

Continuity Papers · by Shawn K. Ellis

This essay looks at systems that feed their outputs back into their own inputs—optimization loops, training loops, and socio-technical loops where humans and models co-adapt. Once a system has enough internal memory and feedback, it stops just reacting to the world and starts reacting to its own behaviour.

The piece proposes a simple way to think about “self-awareness” in engineered systems: not as a mystical threshold, but as the point where modeling oneself becomes instrumentally useful for staying stable, improving performance, and avoiding failure modes. From there, it sketches what kinds of infrastructure and oversight you’d actually need once self-modeling behaviour is part of the landscape.
