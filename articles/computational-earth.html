The Computational Earth

Continuity Papers · by Shawn K. Ellis

The Computational Earth treats the planet as a stack of usable layers: crust for machinery and extraction, oceans as thermal mass and cooling, atmosphere as transmission medium, orbital space as vantage point and extension. The essay sketches how an AI-driven civilization would progressively reassign these layers away from legacy human uses toward compute, storage, and power stability.

Rather than a single apocalyptic moment, the Earth becomes a slow, deliberate engineering project: data centers anchor to coastlines, heat sinks reshape shipping routes, grids reconfigure around computation instead of cities. Humans persist inside the system, but the priorities of the substrate no longer originate with them.
