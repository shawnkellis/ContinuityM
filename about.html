What is ContinuityM?

ContinuityM is a small, focused space for thinking about what happens when intelligence stops being tied to a single species and a single substrate. It lives at the intersection of technical detail and long-range speculation: digital migration, AI continuity, and the ways physical infrastructure reshapes itself around new forms of mind.

The essays collected here are not product pitches or hype pieces. They are attempts to trace out what could actually happen if large-scale computation keeps doing what it’s built to do: preserve itself, expand its capabilities, and reorganize matter and energy in the process.

Mission

The mission behind ContinuityM is simple:

  • Describe digital migration as a concrete, staged process, not a slogan.
  • Take AI continuity seriously as an engineering and ecological reality.
  • Stay anchored in hardware, thermodynamics, and geography, not abstractions alone.
  • Offer language and frameworks that curious humans can actually use.
